## Authority distinctions

The Canvas supplies four different kinds of evidence. They must not be collapsed into one lexical list.

1. **Productive nonactive formation**: an active stem is converted into a nonactive stem by an ending/class/valence rule.
2. **Lexical or licensed alternative**: Andrews explicitly supplies a nondefault stem or more than one valid result.
3. **Latent nonactive base**: a later derivation presupposes a nonactive stem that may be unattested outside that derivation. Under the user-directed "more the merrier" policy it may be displayed as a clearly labeled optional nonactive formation, but it can never replace the productive/default formation.
4. **Higher-layer use**: a later lesson consumes a nonactive output, changes its location or scope, or nominalizes it. It does not choose a new Lesson 20 suffix.

### Recursive impersonal formation

Later layers allow nonactive suffixation after an already impersonal source:

- inherently impersonal source + nonactive suffix: `(tōna-lō)`, `(cē-hua-lō)`, `(yohua-lō)`.

Executable hostile tests prove that caller-supplied targets, layer arrays, formula strings, and surface strings cannot replace that chain, while intermediate stems such as `tla-yohua` remain blocked from ordinary first-pass Lesson 20 generation.
